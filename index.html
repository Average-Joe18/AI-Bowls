Bowls AI

Bowl Filling AI

Bowls AI utilizes a model trained using Google AutoML to decide whether or not a bowl is whether or not a bowl is filled with water. A water control system works in conjunction with the AI to fill the bowl whenever it is found to be empty.

Raspberry PI (RPI)

An RPI acts as the control system for this project. It uses a camera attachment to take pictures periodically, then uses post processsing to make the image more readable for the AI. This includes zooming into the bowl and making the picture brighter. After the AI informs it that the bowl is empty, it sends a signal to the water control system to begin filling the bowl.

AutoML Vision AI

The AutoML Vision API was trained using over a thousand images. All images were taken using the identical camera, from the same height, and with the same bowl. Pictures were taken across multiple times of day to account for different levels of light. For use in the project, the completed model is exported to an RPI.

Water Control System

The water control system is made up of three central componenets: the valve, the power supply, and the switch. When the bowl is full, the RPI does nothing, so the switch keeps the pathway between the power supply and valve open. As long as the valve receves the power supply's 15V signal, it stays closed. Once the RPI detects an empty bowl however, it signals the switch to break the connection between the power supply and valve, causing the valve to open, and the water to flow. The water is stored within a plastic tank at higher elevation than the rest of the system. When the valve opens, gravity moves the water from the tank, through plastic tubing, and into the bowl below.
